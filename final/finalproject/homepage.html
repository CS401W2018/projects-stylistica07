Recipe of the Week

Chicken Adobo

History: Adobo dates back to the pre-colonial era when Filipinos preserved meat in vinegar and salt to prolong its shelf life. When the Spanish arrived, they added soy sauce to the recipe, transforming it into the flavorful dish we know today. [1]

About the Dish: Adobo is versatile, with countless regional variations. Some use coconut milk, while others prefer a dry version. Despite these differences, its tangy and savory taste makes it a Filipino favorite. [2]

A tangy, savory dish made with soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, peppercorn, and bay leaves. Adobo is a beloved classic in Filipino households, perfect for every occasion.
